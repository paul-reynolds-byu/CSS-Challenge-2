Dover Beach by Matthew Arnold

The sea is calm tonight; The tide is full, the moon lies fair

Upon the straits; on the French coast the light; Gleams and is gone;the cliffs of England stand,

Glimmering and vast, out in the tranquil bay; Come to the window, sweet is the night air!

Only, from the long lin of spray; Where the sea meets the moon-blanched land,

Listen! you hear the grating roar; Of pebbles which the waves draw back, and fling,

At their return, up the high strand,
Begin, and cease, and then again begin,

With tremulous cadence slow, and bring; The eternal note of sadness in.
